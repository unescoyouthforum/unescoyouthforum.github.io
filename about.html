About Us

This is what we do.

Following the resolution of the 32nd General Conference held in Paris in 2003, the Kenya National Commission for UNESCO (KNATCOM-UNESCO) Youth Forum was launched in January 2006 as a cooperation of the Kenya National Commission for UNESCO, the Kenyan Youth, the Government of Kenya and the UNESCO headquarters

The aim of the Kenya National Commission for UNESCO Youth Forum is to perpetuate UNESCOâ€™s mandate of building peace in the minds of men and women through youth participation in interdisciplinary and multiplicative approaches to national problems

Purpose

The purpose of the KNATCOM-UNESCO Youth Forum is to contribute to the peace and security of Kenya through education, science and culture in order to further respect for justice, human rights, fundamental freedoms and also to help empower the youth of Kenya. The organization endeavors to promote collaboration amongst youths from all nations..


We seek Peace, Knowing that peace is the climate of freedom

Ibrahim Muhammed Bulle, Education Committee chairperson

Description of what we do

  1. Promote and embrace the concept of peace amongst the youths in Kenya.
  2. Promote human rights awareness amongst the youth in Kenya.
  3. Implement creative ideas and strategies in the form of projects and activities in Kenya.
  4. Represent the youth in Kenya.
  5. Develop and implement leadership programmes to help empower the youth.
  6. Partner with other youths all over the world.
  7. Conduct research, advocate and influence policy in issues affecting young people.
  8. Promote networking and exchange programs with organizations and individuals who are committed to giving the youth a voice and an opportunity.
